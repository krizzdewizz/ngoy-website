For quite a long time now I'm working as a 'full-stack' developer with main emphasis on UI development. We were adopting Angular technology from the very early days until now and never regret it. It's a perfect fit for our company and also for my private work.

Most of our time we are working on SPAs for mobile/web. Now and then we get a requirement from our customers to implement things like a 'sign-up confirmation HTML email' or maybe a downloadable, real-time Excel XML report. Requirements were it is best to go for server-side rendering.

And that's the problem: We have to learn/remember two totally different rendering engines, syntaxes etc. It's a pain to do exactly the same thing, producing HTML, in totally different ways. I wish I had reusable components/classes with a well defined API which I could just throw in and use.

In my personal opinion, all the major JVM template engines out there cannot compete with Angular's component design.

I wanted to have the same experience on the server side and so ngoy was born.

ngoy is pronounced 'enjoy'.

Angular developers should be familiar with ngoy from the very beginning. Binding syntax, components, templates, pipes, services etc. are all the same.

Anyone is welcome to try it out and to see that the modern SPA concept fits very well a 'classic' server-side environment.

I'd like to call it 'SSSPA': server side single page apps.

I know it's nothing new...like most of todays modern software. But it's a fresh look at server-side rendering, which is still heavily used in the industrie.
